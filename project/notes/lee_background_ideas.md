# Lee's Background Ideas

## Setting: Made-up California City

- **Redwood Heights** - Elevated area near redwoods
- **Timber Creek** - Creek running through redwood area

## Job/Profession Ideas

### Option 1: Park Ranger / Forest Guide
**Pros:**
- Natural reason to be in forest frequently
- Knowledge of local area
- Comfortable outdoors
- Explains why he's alone in forest
- Can be nerdy about nature
- Access to remote areas

**Cons:**
- Might be too outdoorsy for sci-fi nerd stereotype
- Could work if he's the "nerdy ranger"

**Character Fit:**
- "I became a ranger because I read too much Tolkien"
- Knows forest but from guidebook perspective
- More interested in fantasy forests than real ones
- Gets to make jokes about his job

### Option 2: Librarian
**Pros:**
- Perfect for sci-fi/fantasy nerd
- Surrounded by books
- Quiet, thoughtful profession
- Explains knowledge and curiosity
- Can be socially awkward
- Flexible hours for forest visits

**Cons:**
- Less natural reason to be in forest alone
- Might seem too stereotypical

**Character Fit:**
- Lives near forest, visits for peace/thinking
- Reads about nature, doesn't experience it much
- First contact could be during rare forest walk
- "I read about forests, I don't usually walk in them"

### Option 3: Freelance Writer / Blogger
**Pros:**
- Works from home
- Flexible schedule
- Can write about sci-fi/fantasy
- Explains isolation and alone time
- Natural reason to explore for inspiration
- Can be anywhere

**Cons:**
- Might be struggling financially
- Could be clich√©

**Character Fit:**
- Writes sci-fi/fantasy blog or reviews
- Goes to forest for inspiration/writing
- "I write about imaginary worlds, turns out real one is weirder"
- Can document journey (internal monologue)

### Option 4: IT Support / Software Developer
**Pros:**
- Nerdy profession
- Can work remotely
- Good income
- Explains tech knowledge
- Socially awkward fits
- Flexible hours

**Cons:**
- Less connection to nature
- Might not explain forest visits

**Character Fit:**
- Works from home
- Goes to forest to escape screens
- "I stare at code all day, trees are a nice change"
- Tech knowledge could be useful later

### Option 5: Museum Curator / Archivist (Natural History)
**Pros:**
- Combines nerd interests with nature
- Knowledge of history and science
- Access to information
- Explains curiosity
- Respectable profession

**Cons:**
- Might be too specialized
- Less flexible schedule

**Character Fit:**
- Works with fossils, ancient history
- Irony of studying ancient past, meeting ancient beings
- "I catalog dead things, now I'm meeting living ancient ones"
- Knowledge of extinction events relevant

### Option 6: Community College Teacher (Literature/Creative Writing)
**Pros:**
- Explains love of stories
- Flexible schedule (summers off)
- Comfortable with learning/teaching
- Can be nerdy and relatable
- Good income but not wealthy

**Cons:**
- Might be too old (teachers often older)
- Could limit age range

**Character Fit:**
- Teaches creative writing, loves sci-fi/fantasy
- Uses forest for inspiration
- "I teach people to write stories, now I'm in one"
- Can analyze story structure of his own life

## Recommended Combination

### Best Fit: Freelance Writer/Blogger
**Why:**
- Most flexible for story needs
- Explains sci-fi/fantasy knowledge
- Natural internal monologue (he's a writer)
- Can be any age (20s-30s ideal)
- Explains alone time and forest visits
- Struggling but passionate
- Relatable to readers

**Details:**
- Writes sci-fi/fantasy blog/reviews
- Small but dedicated following
- Supplements with freelance writing gigs
- Lives modestly near redwood forest
- Goes to forest for inspiration and peace
- First contact happens during writing retreat/walk

**Character Depth:**
- Writes about imaginary worlds
- Discovers real world is stranger
- Can document journey mentally
- Meta-humor about being in a story
- "This would make a great novel... oh wait"
